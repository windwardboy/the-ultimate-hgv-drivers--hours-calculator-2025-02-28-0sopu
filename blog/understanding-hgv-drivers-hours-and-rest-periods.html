Understanding HGV Drivers' Hours and Rest Periods

February 28, 2024 | 10 minute read

HGV drivers must navigate complex regulations regarding driving hours and rest periods. Understanding these rules is crucial for safety, compliance, and avoiding penalties.

Daily Driving Limits

The maximum daily driving time is 9 hours, though this can be extended to 10 hours twice per week. Daily driving time is calculated within a 24-hour period from when you start work after taking a daily or weekly rest period.

Weekly and Fortnightly Limits

Drivers must not exceed 56 hours of driving in a single week (Monday to Sunday). Additionally, the total driving time across any two consecutive weeks must not exceed 90 hours. These limits help prevent fatigue and ensure road safety.

Required Rest Periods

  • Daily rest period: Minimum 11 consecutive hours (can be reduced to 9 hours up to three times between weekly rest periods)
  • Split daily rest: Can be taken in two periods - first at least 3 hours, second at least 9 hours
  • Weekly rest period: Minimum 45 consecutive hours
  • Reduced weekly rest: Minimum 24 consecutive hours (compensation required)

Break Requirements

After driving for 4.5 hours, drivers must take a break of at least 45 minutes. This can be split into two periods:

  • First break: At least 15 minutes
  • Second break: At least 30 minutes

Record Keeping Requirements

Drivers must maintain accurate records of their hours using a digital tachograph. This includes:

  • Recording all driving time, other work, and rest periods
  • Keeping records for at least 28 days
  • Regular downloading of driver card data
  • Maintaining manual records when necessary

Penalties for Non-Compliance

Violations of drivers' hours rules can result in serious consequences:

  • Fixed penalties up to £300
  • Court fines up to £5,000
  • Operator license implications
  • Potential prison sentences for serious offenses

Pro Tips for Compliance

  • Plan routes with appropriate rest stops
  • Use a reliable hours calculator
  • Keep accurate and up-to-date records
  • Stay informed about regulation changes
