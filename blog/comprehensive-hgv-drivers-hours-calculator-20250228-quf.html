February 28, 2024 | 15 minute read

Managing HGV drivers' hours is a critical aspect of ensuring road safety, compliance with regulations, and the well-being of drivers. With the introduction of digital tachographs and advanced tools like the HGV drivers hours calculator, fleet managers and drivers can now streamline this process more efficiently than ever before.

Understanding HGV Drivers Hours Regulations

HGV drivers are subject to strict regulations regarding their working hours to prevent fatigue-related accidents. These rules are enforced by the European Union (EU) and the UK's Driver and Vehicle Standards Agency (DVSA).

  • Daily driving limit: Maximum 9 hours, extendable to 10 hours twice weekly
  • Weekly driving limit: Maximum 56 hours
  • Fortnightly driving limit: Maximum 90 hours over two consecutive weeks
  • Mandatory breaks after 4.5 hours of driving

The Role of Digital Tachographs

Digital tachographs are electronic devices installed in HGVs to record driving time, speed, and distance. Key features include:

  • Automatic recording of driving time and breaks
  • Data storage on both digi card and tachograph unit
  • Compliance monitoring capabilities
  • Tamper-proof design for data integrity

Best Practices for Compliance

  • Use digital tools for automation
  • Provide comprehensive driver training
  • Monitor data regularly
  • Plan schedules strategically
  • Stay updated on regulatory changes
